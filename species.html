About Species

Exotanks are an Earth-Adapted subspecies of Vaui

TLDR: Alien fellas with cool fashionable tanks with a funky reproduction process (no nsfw) P.S I love genetics so I go into detail with it, I don’t mind if u don’t care or understand, I’ll always have simple explanations that way everyone can still have fun creating exos and vauis

It all starts with the Vaui. These Vaui are extraterrestrial creatures that roam the everlasting galaxies. One day the came across planet earth, and as usual, send a team to live and study on the planet. Although, they got curious in the art of glass making and after 400 years of glass making and adaptation to earths ways of living, they started to create tanks for their faces, chests, and tails to better contain their dust. This dust eventually seeps in to evolve into a brain and other organs to better suit earth. (See evolution chart for more info) To add onto these tanks, they eventually started their own “trends” just like the humans, and started decorating their tanks primarily with water but sometimes items, minerals and so much more.

Exotanks primarily take on the shapes of land mammals but have adapted traits for marine life, insectss, and even Dinosaurs! These traits aren’t common but possible. (See genetic typing for more info) Exotanks can also develop mutations, causing them to have multiple limbs, stretched bodies, hair loss, and more. These mutations are usually noticeable around 1 year of age.

When Exotanks are born they do not have any tanks, it’s their parents job to create these tanks and place them in their slots before 1 year or else the body will reject it. Due to the evolution this could cause the youngling to not survive in most cases, but in other cases they can survive but are heavily prone to infections since their soft tissues are mostly unprotected.

This species do not have any reproductive organs. Offspring are produced by one parent starting the cycle of mitosis, but before the offspring develops too much, the 2nd parent (if desired) will transfer the offspring to their own body to finish the process. This way their genetics can morph and cause unique offspring and helps push evolution and development even further. And since they have adapted well to this process, there is usually no risk of loosing offspring. These species don’t technically have genders so there is no restrictions on couples/parents
